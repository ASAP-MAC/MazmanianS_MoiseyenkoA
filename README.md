# Project description

We are interested in understanding if the addition of potentially beneficial microbes
shifts the microbial community. More specifically, does the presence of these microbes
increase abundance of other beneficial commensals and reduce potentially pathogenic
microbes? Is Fp alone able to similarly shift the community as compared to benCom
consortium, or does benCom provide a more robust effect?

In two separate experiments, Wildtype (WT) and alpha-synuclein overexpressing (ASO)
animals were treated with a Vehicle gavage (PBS with sodium bicarbonate) or bacterial
culture to assess effects of commensal microbes typically found to be decreased in
Parkinson’s disease patients on Parkinson's-like symptoms of motor and GI dysfunction
in our mouse model. Treated ASO animals in one experimental cohort received a
gavage of F. prausnitzii ('Fp7' samples), which is an abundant member of the healthy
human gut microbiome residing in the colon and a major producer of butyrate. Treated
animals in a second experimental cohort received a cocktail of 8 commensal microbes,
(which includes Fp) all of which have also been shown to be reduced in the human PD
microbiome and all major producers of SCFAs, named “benCom” for beneficial
commensal community ('bCL' samples). All animals were gavaged with vehicle or
culture 2 times/week starting at 5 weeks of age until 22 weeks of age. At 20-21 wks,
animals were tested for motor and GI function. Both experiments showed improvements
in both areas compared to ASO mice that received a vehicle gavage.

All animals had ad lib water and food. Chow was the same across all cohorts. All animal
handling and tests were done in a biosafety cabinet. Fp fecal pellets were collected less
than 24 hrs after the last gavage. benCom fecal pellets were collected 2 days after last
gavage. All pellets were collected in a biosafety cabinet, fresh, in the morning time by
placing animals into sterile plastic cups, waiting for a pellet to be produced, then
scooping the pellet with a sterile tube and flash freezing and storing at -80 until DNA
extraction. All samples were collected by and processed by the same experimenter.
